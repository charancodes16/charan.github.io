Looking ahead, my research lies at the intersection of AI and HCI, guided by the scientific vision of “Augmenting human intellect using machine intelligence.” I explore: 1) building social computing systems involving agents, simulations, and humans, 2) learning to use systems effectively, and 3) designing novel interfaces with minimal information.

I’m advised by Dr. John M. Carroll—fondly known as “Jack Carroll,” one of the founding fathers of HCI. I’ve also had the opportunity to meet and work with Dr. Frank Ritter (an Allen Newell student), Luke Zhang, Kenneth Huang, among others at the College of IST. As a graduate researcher in the Collaboration Innovation Laboratory under Jack Carroll, I’ve conducted studies in human-computer interaction, cooperative activity, synergies, and interface design. My work is currently in progress and has yet to be published in HCI venues like DIS and MobileHCI.
