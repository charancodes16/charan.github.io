Technology Design for New Parent Couples

Overview. New parents experience decreased relationship closeness while managing childcare demands, with existing technologies primarily supporting logistics rather than emotional connection. Through co-design sessions with 10 Taiwanese couples with young children, we explored current technology use and brainstormed solutions, revealing that technology can foster parental closeness by supporting both interdependence (competence-building, task-sharing) and positive emotional experiences (validation, appreciation, shared joy). Our solution, ParentTogether, is a collaborative parenting platform that integrates practical coordination with relationship-nurturing features to help maintain couple closeness during early parenthood.

Documentation
↑

Exploring how technology can support closeness between new parents while they collaborate on childcare. UX Research, Design Methodologies, Interface. 10 cohabiting Taiwanese couples (first child < 4 years old).

🔍Problem

While couples navigate the demands of early parenthood, their closeness often decreases. Existing technologies primarily support logistics, rather than relationship maintenance.

✏️Research Questions

  • How do couples currently use technology in parental collaboration?
  • How can technologies be designed or reimagined to support closeness in co-parenting?

🧭Approach & Method

  • Semi-structured interviews about current technology use
  • Co-design activities to envision new technology solutions
  • Scenario-based brainstorming using design probes
  • Qualitative methodology

👥Participant Profile

  • 10 cohabiting Taiwanese couples
  • First child younger than 4 years old
  • 5 couples practiced equal caregiving; 5 had the mother as primary caregiver

💞Understanding Closeness

Components of Closeness

  • Shared experiences (spending time together)
  • Interdependence (relying on each other)
  • Intimacy (self-disclosure and responsiveness)

Barriers to Closeness

  • Sleep deprivation and exhaustion
  • Imbalanced childcare responsibilities
  • Lack of time for couple activities
  • Communication challenges

📱Participant Technology Use

Photo Sharing

  • Baby photo collections, sharing with family
  • Limited couple-focused memory creation

To-Do Lists

  • Household/childcare task management
  • Focused on efficiency, lacks recognition

Messaging Apps

  • Childcare coordination
  • Sharing baby info, but no emotional facilitation

Calendars

  • Scheduling and coordination
  • No emotional well-being integration

Baby Activity Tracking Apps

  • Track feeding, sleep, milestones
  • Single-user design, limited sharing

🔍Key Findings

1. Information and Tasks

Existing tools focus on efficiency and logistics, but miss opportunities to build emotional connection through shared responsibility.

2. Emotional Awareness

Few technologies help couples share feelings or recognize signs of stress and exhaustion in their partners during the parenting journey.

3. Positive Experiences

Technologies rarely create opportunities for positive shared experiences or celebration of co-parenting achievements.

🧱Design Frameworks

Interdependence

Design approaches that encourage mutual reliance between partners, highlighting shared goals and complementary strengths in parenting.

Positive Emotions

Features that deliberately foster appreciation, validation, and shared joy in the parenting journey to strengthen emotional bonds.

💡Design Rationale

Task Exchange

  • Flexible system for negotiating childcare responsibilities
  • Acknowledges completed tasks for appreciation and recognition

Connect Prompts

  • Contextual suggestions for couple activities
  • Creates opportunities for playful interaction

Emotion Check-In

  • Discreet mood tracking
  • Offers tailored emotional support suggestions

Parenting Memory Wall

  • Digital repository of parenting milestones
  • Transforms routines into meaningful memories

Shared Dashboard

  • Real-time baby tracking with equal editing access
  • Visualizes both parents' contributions

🧩Design Implications

For Technology Designers

  • Consider both logistical and emotional needs
  • Design for equal participation of both parents
  • Integrate closeness-building features into tools

For Family Support Systems

  • Recognize technology's role in family relationships
  • Consider cultural contexts in design
  • Address both practical and relational co-parenting needs

🔮Future Work

Design Refinement

  • Prototype development with user testing
  • Longitudinal study on couple closeness impact

Research Extensions

  • Include diverse family structures (same-sex, non-cohabiting)
  • Cross-cultural comparisons
  • Integration with extended family caregiving networks
