About. Charan is a Master's student in Informatics (Human-Computer Interaction) at Pennsylvania State University's College of IST, advised by Dr. Jack Carroll. At the Collaboration Innovation Laboratory, he researches cooperative activity, synergies, and interface design. He has collaborated with Dr. Frank Ritter, Luke Zhang, and Kenneth Huang. His work is targeted for publication in HCI venues like DIS and MobileHCI. Previously, Charan earned his Bachelor's in Computer Science from Kumaraguru College of Technology (2023) and gained industry experience through internships at HDFC Bank, Freecharge, and as a founding designer at an angel-backed fintech startup.

Overview. New parents experience decreased relationship closeness while managing childcare demands, with existing technologies primarily supporting logistics rather than emotional connection. Through co-design sessions with 10 Taiwanese couples with young children, we explored current technology use and brainstormed solutions, revealing that technology can foster parental closeness by supporting both interdependence (competence-building, task-sharing) and positive emotional experiences (validation, appreciation, shared joy). Our solution, ParentTogether, is a collaborative parenting platform that integrates practical coordination with relationship-nurturing features to help maintain couple closeness during early parenthood.

Overview. Penn State University students faced significant challenges discovering campus events due to information being scattered across multiple platforms (department websites, social media, emails, and physical posters), leading to missed opportunities and frustration. Our solution, Event Aggregator, addresses this problem through two integrated platforms: Gather, a mobile app for attendees that centralizes event discovery with personalized recommendations based on interests, and Post, a web tool for organizers that streamlines event creation and updates.
